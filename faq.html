Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a smart contract?

    The term was first coined by Nick Szabo in 1994. Smart contracts are one of the most exciting applications of blockchain technology. The blockchain is what makes these digital contracts smart; blockchain technology stores, verifies and executes the contracts so that there is never any question of the contract’s validity, date, or existence.
    Consider wagering on the outcome of a sporting match directly with another bettor where the results of the game automatically trigger a payout. Results are validated by all computers on the blockchain network and winning party is credited with their stake plus winnings.

  • Why would I use a smart contract?

    A smart contract is a cost-effective digital agreement that will leave no ambiguity.
    Blockchain technology stores, verifies and executes smart contracts so there is never any question of the contract’s validity, date, or existence.

  • What are the benefits?

    Peer-to-Peer or Business-to-User smart contracts offer greater degree of control within private matters. The cost is low, and can be paid by fiat and digital currency.

  • How does Etherparty use smart contracts?

    Etherparty manages, creates, and executes smart contracts, without the hassle of setting up and maintaining network requirements.

  • How does Etherparty make using smart contracts easy?

    Etherparty will allow users to compile and deploy smart contracts without any coding knowledge. By using a front-end for smart contract creation, writing a smart contract is as simple as filling out a form. The platform removes the complexity and technical programming involved with smart contract creation by implementing visual tools and a simple interface.

  • How much does it cost to run a smart contract?

    Fuel tokens are used and the cost varies depending on the size and duration of the smart contract.
